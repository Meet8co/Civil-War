Guide for New Civil War Soldiers

Union and Confederate Army Orientation

Training Uniforms Health & Hygiene Battles Camp Life

Training

Soldiers received limited training, mostly focused on marching, using rifles, and following orders. Drills were repetitive and discipline was strict to prepare men for battle.

  • Drill sessions could last for hours daily.
  • Recruits learned battlefield formations and commands.
  • Many soldiers had no prior military experience.

Uniforms

Union soldiers typically wore blue uniforms while Confederate soldiers wore gray or butternut. Uniforms varied based on supply availability and rank.

  • Uniforms were often ill-fitting or mismatched.
  • Confederates sometimes wore civilian clothing due to shortages.
  • Insignias indicated rank and unit.

Health & Hygiene

Sanitary conditions were poor. Disease caused more deaths than battle. Soldiers often lacked clean water, proper food, and medical care. Lice and dysentery were common.

  • Common diseases: dysentery, typhoid fever, pneumonia.
  • Amputations were a frequent medical procedure.
  • Field hospitals were overcrowded and under-equipped.

Battles

Battles were intense and chaotic, involving long marches, artillery, and bayonet charges. Popular battle strategies included flanking and entrenchment.

  • Major battles included Gettysburg, Antietam, and Shiloh.
  • Commanders played critical roles in strategy and morale.
  • Communication was limited to runners, flags, and bugle calls.

Camp Life

Camp life was often boring and uncomfortable. Soldiers passed time with letters, cards, music, and storytelling. Living quarters were tents or makeshift shelters.

  • Food typically consisted of hardtack, salt pork, and beans.
  • Letters from home were a major morale booster.
  • Music and campfire stories were common entertainment.
